About me

I’m Adrian Alamo, a recent graduate from the University of California, Berkeley, where I earned a B.A. in Computer Science with a Minor in Chicanx/Latinx Studies. Born and raised in Westlake, Los Angeles, I’ve built my path around curiosity, persistence, and a passion for creating technology that drives meaningful impact.

My technical expertise spans software development, data science, and computer security, with proficiency in Java, C, Python, and assembly. I’ve applied these skills through hands-on projects—most notably developing ARES, an augmented reality app showcasing UC Berkeley’s political history, in collaboration with Professor Pablo Gonzalez and the Ethnic Studies Changemaker Studios.

Previously, I interned at CreateMe Technologies, a San Francisco robotics startup, where I contributed to automation and simulation software, built a full-stack Flask web application, and worked with object detection, deep reinforcement learning, and tools like Azure, Git, and Docker.

I currently serve as the Data Science Lead at SingleMLT, an early-stage startup I co-founded, where I design database architecture and develop machine learning models for personalized user recommendations. I’m passionate about leveraging technology to build innovative, data-driven systems that bridge creativity and impact.

I’m now eager to apply my technical skills to impactful projects in software development and machine learning. I look forward to connecting with like-minded professionals who are passionate about using technology to drive innovation.
